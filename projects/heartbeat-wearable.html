Would You Understand

A wearable that transmits heartbeats as haptic vibrations, exploring intimate connection through embodied experience rather than visual or audio channels.

Course: Design Studio | Timeline: 12 weeks | Team: Solo | Status: Prototype (functional)

Technologies: Arduino Mega 2560, HC-05 Bluetooth, custom vibrotactile motors, soft silicone, rPPG

The Question

How can we create intimate, meaningful connections without relying on words, faces, or physical presence? Most connection technology focuses on sight and sound. But our bodies communicate through touch, rhythm, and timing. What if heartbeats—the most fundamental rhythm of life—could become a channel for presence?

The Approach

Rather than showing data about heartbeats, I wanted to create direct feeling—translating one person's heart rhythm into haptic vibrations felt by another wearing the device. This required solving three major challenges:

Challenge 1: Contactless Detection - Wired sensors break intimacy. Solution: Used smartphone camera-based rPPG detecting blood flow color changes in fingertips.

Challenge 2: Smooth Haptics - Raw sensor-to-motor mapping created erratic vibrations. Solution: Temporal smoothing and beat interpolation for steady pulses.

Challenge 3: Comfort - Motors are heavy. Solution: Soft silicone housing with careful weight distribution for 30+ minute wear.

Technical Implementation

Hardware: Arduino Mega 2560, HC-05 Bluetooth, custom vibrotactile actuators, soft silicone enclosure, 3 wearable units

Software: Real-time rPPG algorithm, 30Hz signal processing, beat interpolation engine, PWM motor control

Process & Iteration

Weeks 1-3: Direct sensor-to-motor mapping felt erratic and artificial.

Week 4 Breakthrough: Discovered that consistency matters more than accuracy. A steady 72 BPM felt human. Jittery 72-79-71 BPM felt wrong.

Weeks 5-7: Built temporal smoothing to create physiologically plausible rhythms.

Weeks 8-10: User testing with 5 testers. Device was comfortable but shifted during movement. Redesigned enclosure.

Weeks 11-12: Final paired testing revealed spontaneous breathing synchronization after 2-3 minutes. Users felt "less like data, more like presence."

Outcome & Reflection

Successfully created intimate embodied presence. User feedback highlighted:

  • Feeling remarkably present despite no visual/audio contact
  • Natural breathing and posture synchronization
  • Unexpected emotional closeness from haptic interface

Key Insight: The best interfaces disappear—operating at the edge of perception. Constraint created meaning. The limitations of rPPG forced smoothing and interpretation that became more emotionally resonant than clinical accuracy.

Next Steps: Explore multi-person heartbeats, longer-term psychological effects, and whether sustained use changes empathy or connection.
